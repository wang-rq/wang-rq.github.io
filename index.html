    • Research Projects

    Artificial Intelligence in Healthcare

    • Multi-modal survival analysis using modalities of different importance. [May 2021 ~ Aug 2021]
      Machine Perception Laboratory, SYSU, Guangzhou, China
      My contributions:
      • Designed an asymmetrical multi-modal attention mechanism (AMMA) to generate the more flexible joint representation of medical images and structured data.
      • Designed and conducted various experiments to verify the effectiveness of the new model. On different types of cancer data sets, the results of the method are 5%-6% higher (C-index) than other SOTA methods.
      • Paper: [AMMASurv-Paper]. (IEEE BIBM under review.)

    • Integration of Patch Features of Whole Slide Images. [Dec 2020 ~ Mar 2021]
      Machine Perception Laboratory, SYSU, Guangzhou, China
      My contributions:
      • Researched the influence of self-supervised learning on whole slide image (WSI) feature extraction and the effect of position embedding of WSI patches.
      • Our approach outperforms the previous best approach by an average of 3% (C-index) on three datasets.
      • Paper: [SeTranSurv-Paper]. (Accepted by MICCAI 2021.)

    • The effect of surgery and drug treatment on the visual field progression of different glaucoma patients and glaucoma patients with comorbidities.[Dec 2020 ~ Mar 2021]
      Zhongshan Ophthalmic Center, Guangzhou, China
      My contributions:
      • Designed an efficient sample matching method based on the Levenshtein distance algorithm, which increased the number of effective samples by 30%, facilitating subsequent experiments.
      • The results will be published in the future.

    • Machine disease diagnosis using on small and unbalanced datasets with multi-modal data.[Nov 2020 ~ Dec 2020]
      Machine Perception Laboratory, SYSU, Guangzhou, China
      My contributions:
      • Analyzed clinical data and omics data in patients with nasopharyngeal carcinoma and use multi-modal data to get better joint representations for classification. Proved the complementarity between data from two modalities.
      • Studied the application of machine learning methods to small and uneven datasets.
      • Cooperated with SYSU Cancer Center (SYSUCC).
